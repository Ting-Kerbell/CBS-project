What exactly is a Meme?

According to the Oxford English Dictionary, a meme is “an element of a culture that may be considered to be passed on by non-genetic means, esp. Imitation”. Humans are incredible imitators; this means that we can “copy ideas, habits, skills, behaviours, inventions, song and stories” (https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2212683X15000444 ← actual citation later). Even from birth, infants learn from observing and imitating the actions from adults (https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2007/12/071205102433.htm). Furthermore, memes are a cultural expression. Like viruses, they are pieces of information that pervade a group or society, often spreading rapidly and are frequently being copied into memory. With the diversity amongst different social groups, different memes affect each individual in a distinct way. Thus, different groups of people have different memes, showing that memes contribute very much to social cohesion.
